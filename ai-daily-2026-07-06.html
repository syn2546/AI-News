EU Council Formally Approves "Digital Omnibus" Delaying AI Act Deadlines

›
Published 2026-06-29

The Council of the European Union gave its final approval to the "Digital Omnibus on AI," officially delaying key compliance deadlines for the EU AI Act. The application date for standalone high-risk AI systems is deferred from August 2026 to December 2, 2027, while high-risk systems embedded in regulated products are delayed to August 2028. However, the legislation accelerates deadlines for prohibitions on AI-generated non-consensual intimate media and child abuse material to December 2, 2026.

Why it matters: Regulators are conceding to industry pressure by granting companies a 16-month compliance extension to build harmonized standards, while keeping transparency rules on a fast track.
