EU Parliament Delays Critical High-Risk Deadlines for EU AI Act Compliance

›
Published 2026-06-16

The European Parliament approved a legislative package called the "Digital Omnibus" that delays compliance deadlines for high-risk AI systems. Stand-alone high-risk AI software compliance is pushed to December 2, 2027, while high-risk systems embedded in physical products are delayed to August 2, 2028. However, transparency rules requiring companies to label deepfakes and disclose chatbot interactions remain active for August 2, 2026.

Why it matters: The delay gives developers breathing room to navigate compliance burdens, preventing regulatory overhead from choking off early-stage AI investment and deployment in Europe.
