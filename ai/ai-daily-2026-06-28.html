›
Published 2026-06-16

The European Parliament gave final approval to amendments delaying core obligations of the EU AI Act, slipping the start date for standalone high-risk systems (employment, credit scoring, critical infrastructure, law enforcement) from August 2026 to 2 December 2027 — a 16-month reprieve driven by missing technical standards. Why it matters: The world's most aggressive AI rulebook just blinked, buying global vendors over a year of breathing room and signaling Brussels is bending toward competitiveness over speed of enforcement.
