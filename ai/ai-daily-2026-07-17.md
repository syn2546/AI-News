# AI Daily — Friday, July 17, 2026

Coverage: the latest verified developments available this week through July 17, Bangkok time (UTC+07:00).

## 🧠 Top 10 AI News

1. **Moonshot releases Kimi K3, a 3T-class open model built for agentic work** 🚀 Launch
   Moonshot’s official API documentation announces Kimi K3 as a formally released flagship model for software engineering, knowledge work, and deep reasoning. The model supports a 1M-token context window, visual input, custom and dynamically loaded tools, and official tool calling.
   Why it matters: K3 brings long-context, tool-using agent capabilities into an openly available model and API, increasing pressure on frontier-model pricing and accessibility.
   Reference: https://platform.kimi.com/docs/guide/kimi-k3-quickstart

2. **Mistral introduces Robostral Navigate for single-camera robot navigation** 🚀 Launch
   Mistral introduced Robostral Navigate, an 8B model that uses a single RGB camera and a natural-language instruction to move robots through complex environments. Mistral reports 76.6% success on unseen R2R-CE benchmarks and positions the system as a lower-sensor route into embodied AI.
   Why it matters: If the result transfers beyond the company’s evaluation, robotics developers can reduce sensor and hardware requirements for visual navigation.
   Reference: https://mistral.ai/news/robostral-navigate/

3. **NVIDIA launches Nemotron 3 Embed models for agentic retrieval** 🚀 Launch
   NVIDIA released three open-weight embedding models—an 8B flagship and two 1B variants—for RAG, code retrieval, and agent memory. NVIDIA reports that the 8B model ranks first on RTEB, while the smaller models target lower-cost and Blackwell-optimized production deployments; the benchmark claims are vendor-reported.
   Why it matters: Better, cheaper retrieval directly improves the reliability, latency, and token economics of multi-step AI agents.
   Reference: https://huggingface.co/blog/nvidia/nemotron-3-embed-wins-rteb

4. **Google begins rolling out connected apps inside AI Mode in Search** 🚀 Launch
   Google is rolling out integrations that let users connect services such as Instacart, Canva, and YouTube Music directly to AI Mode in Search. The integrations allow AI Mode to perform actions such as adding groceries to a cart, finding design templates, and creating playlists without leaving Search.
   Why it matters: Search is moving from answering questions toward taking actions across users’ third-party applications.
   Reference: https://blog.google/products-and-platforms/products/search/connected-apps/

5. **Google DeepMind and Isomorphic Labs publish a joint AI bioresilience approach** 🔬 Research
   Google DeepMind and Isomorphic Labs outlined how frontier models and agents could support biological-threat prevention, outbreak detection, and response while controlling misuse. The approach includes threat modeling and monitoring for Gemini, exploration of biology-aware SynthID screening, and use of AlphaEvolve to optimize metagenomic analysis; the claims and program results come from the companies.
   Why it matters: It frames advanced AI safety and biological resilience as a combined engineering problem spanning misuse prevention and scientific response capacity.
   Reference: https://deepmind.google/blog/our-approach-to-bioresilience/

6. **Fireworks raises $1.505 billion Series D at a $17.5 billion valuation** 💰 Funding
   Fireworks announced a $1.505 billion Series D led by Atreides Management, Index Ventures, and TCV, with participation from NVIDIA and other existing investors. The company said its annualized revenue run rate has surpassed $1 billion as enterprises increasingly customize open models for proprietary workflows.
   Why it matters: The round signals major investor conviction in enterprise platforms that make open AI models cheaper and more specialized.
   Reference: https://www.otpp.com/en-ca/about-us/news-and-insights/2026/fireworks-raises-a-1-5-billion-series-d-to-lead-the-specialized-intelligence-revolution/

7. **EU orders Google to give rival AI assistants greater Android and Search access** 🏛️ Policy
   The European Union ordered Google to provide rival AI assistants with access to Android capabilities comparable to Gemini and to share selected Google Search data with competing search engines and AI services under the Digital Markets Act. Google has until January 2027 to begin sharing Search data and July 2027 to implement the Android changes.
   Why it matters: The rulings could weaken Google’s distribution advantage in AI assistants and create new openings for ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, and other rivals.
   Reference: https://www.theverge.com/policy/966438/eu-google-android-ai-interoperability-search-data-dma

8. **Intel expands multi-year AI collaboration with Google Cloud across chip design and operations** 🏢 Industry
   Intel and Google Cloud expanded their collaboration, with Intel integrating Gemini Enterprise and Google Cloud infrastructure across engineering, supply chain, and corporate operations. Google Cloud will also augment Intel’s semiconductor-development environment, including cloud-based compute for high-performance chip-design simulations and agentic workflows.
   Why it matters: A major chipmaker is moving AI from pilot programs into core engineering and manufacturing processes, potentially shortening semiconductor development cycles.
   Reference: https://newsroom.intel.com/artificial-intelligence/intel-google-cloud-announce-collaboration-to-accelerate-intel-ai-enabled-enterprise-transformation

9. **3M and Microsoft form a strategic partnership for AI infrastructure and enterprise transformation** 🏢 Industry
   Microsoft will deploy 3M’s Expanded Beam Optical technology in Azure data centers, making it the first announced hyperscale cloud provider to adopt the technology. 3M will also use Microsoft’s AI and digital platforms across customer service, finance, sales, marketing, and an AI-agent workflow for customer-order management.
   Why it matters: The deal links physical data-center bottlenecks with enterprise AI adoption, showing how AI expansion is creating demand well beyond chips and models.
   Reference: https://news.microsoft.com/source/2026/07/15/3m-and-microsoft-announce-strategic-partnership-to-advance-ai-data-center-infrastructure-and-enterprise-transformation/

10. **Whatnot acquires Shaped to improve real-time live-shopping recommendations** 🏢 Industry
    Livestream-shopping platform Whatnot acquired Shaped, a machine-learning company focused on real-time recommendations and search; financial terms were not disclosed. Shaped’s CEO and nearly a dozen engineers and AI researchers will join Whatnot, whose systems process more than 500,000 hours of live video and millions of weekly interactions.
    Why it matters: The acquisition shows AI M&A shifting toward specialized personalization infrastructure for fast-changing commerce environments.
    Reference: https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/15/whatnot-acquires-shaped-to-power-real-time-live-shopping-recommendations/
