# AI Daily — Thursday, July 16, 2026

Coverage: the prior 24 hours through the morning of July 16, Bangkok time (UTC+07:00). Company announcements and reported claims are labeled as such.

## 🧠 Top 10 AI News

1. **Thinking Machines releases Inkling, a trillion-parameter open multimodal model** 🚀 Launch
   Thinking Machines released Inkling under Apache 2.0 with 975B total parameters, 41B active parameters, multimodal text-image-audio input, and a one-million-token context window. Launch-day integrations include Transformers, SGLang, vLLM, and llama.cpp.
   Why it matters: Developers gain an adaptable frontier-scale multimodal foundation outside closed APIs, although its memory footprint confines direct deployment to substantial infrastructure.
   Reference: https://huggingface.co/blog/thinkingmachines-inkling

2. **NVIDIA launches lower-power Jetson Thor modules for production robotics** 🚀 Launch
   NVIDIA unveiled the Blackwell-powered Jetson T3000 and T2000 modules for robots and edge-AI systems, alongside optimization tooling and the 4B Cosmos 3 Edge model. Developers can begin through emulation this month, with commercial modules scheduled for early 2027.
   Why it matters: Smaller Thor systems could move multimodal models and robot policies from centralized compute into mass-deployed autonomous machines.
   Reference: https://blogs.nvidia.com/blog/jetson-thor-robotics-edge-ai-agent/

3. **OpenAI trains GPT-Red to automate adversarial testing** 🔬 Research
   OpenAI introduced GPT-Red, an internal red-teaming model trained through self-play to discover prompt-injection attacks while defender models learn to resist them. OpenAI reports that the resulting adversarial training cut GPT-5.6 Sol failures sixfold on its hardest direct-injection benchmark.
   Why it matters: Automated adversarial training offers a scalable route for agent security to improve alongside model capability rather than lag behind it.
   Reference: https://openai.com/index/unlocking-self-improvement-gpt-red/

4. **Apple Intelligence approved for China launch with Alibaba’s Qwen** 🏢 Industry
   China’s Cyberspace Administration approved Apple’s AI services for the country, backed by an agreement integrating Alibaba’s Qwen models into iOS, iPadOS, macOS, and visionOS. Alibaba confirmed that Qwen would power text and image understanding and generation, though it did not provide a launch date.
   Why it matters: Regulatory clearance opens a major market for Apple’s delayed AI rollout while elevating Qwen as a platform model inside one of the world’s largest device ecosystems.
   Reference: https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/15/apple-intelligence-approved-for-launch-in-china-with-alibabas-qwen-ai/

5. **OpenAI launches a $230 Codex keyboard for managing coding agents** 🚀 Launch
   OpenAI and Work Louder released Codex Micro, a limited-run keyboard with illuminated agent-status keys, workflow shortcuts, and a control for adjusting agent reasoning level. OpenAI told TechCrunch the device is a small collaboration rather than a mass-market product.
   Why it matters: The experiment tests whether dedicated physical controls can make fleets of coding agents easier to supervise, even if this first device is mainly a niche showcase.
   Reference: https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/15/amid-hardware-legal-battle-openai-releases-a-230-keyboard-for-codex/

6. **CXMT draws investor bids for an $8.6B Shanghai IPO** 💰 Funding
   Chinese memory-chip manufacturer CXMT priced an $8.6B IPO at 8.66 yuan per share, implying a 579.18B-yuan valuation. The company plans to use the proceeds to expand production and technology as AI demand lifts memory consumption.
   Why it matters: The offering channels major domestic capital into China’s effort to challenge Samsung, SK Hynix, and Micron in strategically critical AI memory.
   Reference: https://www.tradingview.com/news/reuters.com,2026:newsml_L4N43H063:0-eyeing-valuation-jump-investors-prep-bids-for-chip-giant-cxmt-s-8-6-billion-ipo/

7. **Emergent raises $130M Series C at a $1.5B valuation** 💰 Funding
   TechCrunch reported that Indian AI-coding startup Emergent raised a $130M Series C led by Creaegis at a $1.5B post-money valuation.
   Why it matters: The round is a capital signal for AI application-building platforms aimed at non-technical businesses as well as developers.
   Reference: https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/15/indian-ai-coding-startup-emergent-becomes-a-unicorn-just-over-a-year-after-launch/

8. **Oak exits stealth with $60M for AI-era identity management** 💰 Funding
   Oak emerged from stealth with $60M in seed funding for an identity-management control plane designed to remove unnecessary permissions as application use changes.
   Why it matters: Identity governance is becoming a core enterprise bottleneck as access is delegated to AI agents.
   Reference: https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/15/backed-by-60m-in-funding-oak-steps-out-of-stealth-to-fix-the-identity-mess-that-ai-agents-are-making-worse/

9. **Rime raises $24M Series A for enterprise voice AI** 💰 Funding
   Rime announced a $24M Series A led by M13 Ventures for enterprise voice-AI systems trained on conversational data it records itself.
   Why it matters: The financing backs specialized automation of high-stakes enterprise calls, where accuracy and latency still constrain adoption.
   Reference: https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/15/rime-picks-up-24m-series-a-to-help-enterprises-field-customer-calls/

10. **Anthropic and Blackstone’s $1.5B implementation venture is named Ode** 🏢 Industry
    TechCrunch reported that Ode with Anthropic is the $1.5B AI-implementation venture launched with Anthropic, Blackstone, Hellman & Friedman, Goldman Sachs, and others; it acquired Fractional AI.
    Why it matters: It formalizes a major push toward monetizing enterprise AI implementation services, not only model access.
    Reference: https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/15/anthropic-blackstone-bet-the-next-trillion-dollar-ai-business-is-implementation-not-models/
