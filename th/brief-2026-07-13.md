## 🎙️ Stakeholder Statements (Headhunter)
1. **Vitai Ratanakorn, Governor of the Bank of Thailand** (2026-07-11)
   *Core Message:* Governor Vitai announced new measures to combat the "grey economy," requiring individuals depositing 5 million baht or more in cash to disclose their source of funds starting in Q4 2026. He also warned that Thailand is "missing the boat" on the global AI boom, emphasizing that the country's slow economic growth (forecasted at 2.3%) is constrained by deep-rooted structural problems rather than issues that can be fixed with short-term interest rate cuts.
   *Impact Analysis:* This signals a major tightening of financial compliance for high-net-worth individuals and businesses, while reinforcing the central bank's reluctance to use monetary easing (with rates already at 1.00%) for structural challenges.
   Reference: https://www.nationthailand.com/
2. **Anutin Charnvirakul, Prime Minister** (2026-07-13)
   *Core Message:* Following talks with Singapore's Foreign Minister, Prime Minister Anutin emphasized the need to accelerate the ASEAN Power Grid project to boost regional energy security and connectivity. Earlier in the week, he publicly instructed the Energy Minister to implement measures to reduce domestic retail pump prices, citing recent declines in global oil prices.
   *Impact Analysis:* His statements signal a dual strategy of attempting to provide immediate cost-of-living relief to consumers while strategically positioning Thailand as a central player in Southeast Asian energy infrastructure integration.
   Reference: https://www.thaienquirer.com/
3. **Executive Leadership, Asia Era One Consortium (CP Group-led)** (2026-07-06)
   *Core Message:* The CP Group-led consortium submitted a formal letter to the State Railway of Thailand requesting the termination of the three-airport high-speed rail joint investment contract, citing an inability to secure Board of Investment (BOI) certification. The group subsequently clarified that the move was a necessary procedural step to preserve their contractual rights amid project constraints, rather than an immediate or unilateral withdrawal.
   *Impact Analysis:* This highlights severe regulatory and financial bottlenecks in Thailand's flagship megaprojects, threatening to stall the Eastern Economic Corridor (EEC) development timeline and potentially dampening overall foreign investor confidence.
   Reference: https://www.bangkokpost.com/
