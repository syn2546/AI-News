📰 Top 10 Financial News

Wholesale inflation posted its largest monthly decline since April 2025, while core producer prices rose 0.2% month over month. Annual headline PPI slowed to 5.5% from 6.0%, but remained elevated.

Why it matters: The downside surprise lowered near-term rate-hike expectations and helped pull Treasury yields lower.

↗ Reference

Shipping companies were reportedly avoiding a U.S.-assisted Strait of Hormuz transit route after attacks raised crew-safety concerns. The scheme had helped move tens of millions of barrels through the strait and limited some of the energy-price impact.

Why it matters: Reduced confidence in protected Hormuz passages raises the risk of another oil-price surge, renewed inflation pressure and higher shipping costs.

↗ Reference

During his second day of congressional testimony, Kevin Warsh called the latest CPI and PPI readings encouraging but imperfect measures of underlying inflation and offered few specifics on the Fed’s next move. He reiterated that he would act independently.

Why it matters: Warsh’s caution limits the extent to which markets can translate softer inflation data into a clear shift in the Fed’s policy path.

↗ Reference

Economic activity increased at a slight-to-moderate pace in 11 of the Federal Reserve’s 12 districts, while employment gains broadened. Prices still increased moderately overall, but price growth was unchanged or slower in every district.

Why it matters: The report depicts a resilient economy with persistent but no longer accelerating price pressure, complicating the case for either an imminent hike or easing.

↗ Reference

The S&P 500 gained 0.38%, the Nasdaq advanced 0.62% and the Dow added 0.29% as softer producer-price data and strong corporate results supported risk appetite. The 10-year Treasury yield fell to 4.55%.

Why it matters: Equities responded positively to disinflation and earnings even as oil and geopolitical risks remained a potential constraint on valuations.

↗ Reference

John Williams said inflation remained “unquestionably too high” at about 4%, but he saw encouraging reasons to expect it had peaked and would decline. He called the current 3.50%–3.75% federal-funds target range well positioned.

Why it matters: Williams reinforced the view that another near-term rate increase is unlikely while leaving room for later easing.

↗ Reference

China’s economy grew 4.3% year over year in the April–June quarter, below forecasts and slower than the first quarter’s 5% pace. Strong exports contrasted with weak domestic spending and investment.

Why it matters: Slower Chinese growth threatens global demand and commodity consumption while increasing the likelihood of policy support and trade friction.

↗ Reference

BlackRock reported adjusted second-quarter EPS of $13.91 and revenue of $7.08 billion, up 31% year over year. Assets under management climbed to $15.3 trillion, with $192 billion of quarterly net inflows.

Why it matters: The beat and record assets indicate strong institutional and passive-investment flows across asset management.

↗ Reference

President Trump was set to urge major defense contractors to expand manufacturing capacity as wars in Ukraine and the Middle East depleted U.S. stockpiles. The Pennsylvania summit brought together Pentagon officials, commanders, defense companies, technology firms and investors.

Why it matters: A sustained procurement push could expand order backlogs and capital spending across U.S. defense, aerospace and advanced manufacturing.
