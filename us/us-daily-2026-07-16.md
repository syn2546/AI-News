## 📰 Top 10 Financial News
1. **U.S. producer prices fall 0.3% in June as energy costs retreat** [🏛️ Macro & Policy]
   Wholesale inflation posted its largest monthly decline since April 2025, while core producer prices rose 0.2% month over month. Annual headline PPI slowed to 5.5% from 6.0%, but remained elevated.
   **Why it matters:** The downside surprise lowered near-term rate-hike expectations and helped pull Treasury yields lower.
   Reference: https://apnews.com/article/producer-prices-inflation-wholesale-033764304e871cea56bd0fc501aee294

2. **Developing: Shipping companies refuse some U.S.-military-guided Hormuz transits after attacks** [💰 Markets]
   Shipping companies were reportedly avoiding a U.S.-assisted Strait of Hormuz transit route after attacks raised crew-safety concerns. The scheme had helped move tens of millions of barrels through the strait and limited some of the energy-price impact from the disruption.
   **Why it matters:** Reduced confidence in protected Hormuz passages raises the risk of another oil-price surge, renewed inflation pressure and higher shipping costs.
   Reference: https://www.dawn.com/news/2015696

3. **Fed Chair Warsh gives little rate guidance and downplays two days of cooler inflation data** [🏛️ Macro & Policy]
   During his second day of congressional testimony, Kevin Warsh called the latest CPI and PPI readings encouraging but imperfect measures of underlying inflation and offered few specifics on the Fed’s next move. He reiterated that he would act independently while declining to detail contacts with President Trump.
   **Why it matters:** Warsh’s caution limits the extent to which markets can translate softer inflation data into a clear shift in the Fed’s policy path.
   Reference: https://apnews.com/article/federal-reserve-warsh-inflation-ai-trump-09923713ddb06f25d17c975717e4fb15

4. **Fed Beige Book shows broader growth and employment gains as price growth stops accelerating** [🏛️ Macro & Policy]
   Economic activity increased at a slight-to-moderate pace in 11 of the Federal Reserve’s 12 districts, while employment gains broadened. Prices still increased moderately overall, but price growth was unchanged or slower in every district.
   **Why it matters:** The report depicts a resilient economy with persistent but no longer accelerating price pressure, complicating the case for either an imminent hike or easing.
   Reference: https://www.federalreserve.gov/monetarypolicy/beigebook202607-summary.htm

   **Why it matters:** Williams offered an explicit disinflation forecast, reinforcing the view that another near-term rate increase is unlikely while leaving room for later easing.
